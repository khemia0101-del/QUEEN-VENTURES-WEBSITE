# Job Listing Distribution & Outreach Strategy

## Overview

This document outlines a comprehensive strategy for distributing the Director of Development & Capital Partnerships job listing across multiple channels to reach qualified capital professionals, CDFI leaders, and impact investors.

---

## Primary Distribution Channels

### 1. Professional Networks & Job Boards

**CDFI Coalition (cdfi.org)**
- Post on CDFI Coalition job board
- Reach: CDFI leaders, development professionals, capital operators
- Timeline: Immediate
- Contact: [CDFI Coalition careers/jobs contact]

**Opportunity Finance Network (ofn.org)**
- Post on OFN job board and member network
- Reach: Impact investors, CDFI professionals, community development leaders
- Timeline: Immediate
- Contact: [OFN careers/jobs contact]

**Affordable Housing Online (affordablehousing.com)**
- Post on job board
- Reach: Housing professionals, developers, capital professionals
- Timeline: Immediate
- Cost: Typically $200–500 for 30-day listing

**National Association of Home Builders (NAHB)**
- Post on NAHB job board (if available)
- Reach: Housing developers, builders, development professionals
- Timeline: Immediate

**National Community Development Association (NCDA)**
- Post on member job board
- Reach: Community development professionals
- Timeline: Immediate

### 2. LinkedIn Strategy

**Organic Posts**
- Post about the opportunity on Queen Ventures' LinkedIn company page
- Highlight: mission, platform, compensation structure, why this role matters
- Include link to careers page: queenventures.org/careers
- Frequency: 1–2 posts per week for 4 weeks

**Direct Outreach**
- Identify capital professionals with relevant experience (CDFI leaders, development officers, impact investors)
- Send personalized LinkedIn messages using templates provided
- Target: 50–100 qualified professionals in first round
- Follow-up: 1–2 weeks after initial message

**LinkedIn Ads (Optional)**
- Target capital professionals, CDFI employees, impact investors
- Budget: $500–1,000 for 2–4 weeks
- Call-to-action: "Learn More" or "Apply Now" linking to careers page

### 3. Email Campaigns

**CDFI Network Outreach**
- Compile list of CDFI leaders, development officers, and capital professionals
- Send personalized emails using templates provided
- Include link to careers page and application instructions
- Timeline: Week 1–2
- Follow-up: Week 3 if no response

**Foundation & Impact Investor Outreach**
- Identify foundations and impact investors focused on affordable housing, community development
- Send targeted emails highlighting mission alignment and opportunity
- Timeline: Week 1–2
- Follow-up: Week 3–4

**Board & Advisor Referral Requests**
- Email Queen Ventures board members and advisors with referral request
- Include job summary and application link
- Ask for introductions to qualified candidates in their networks
- Timeline: Immediate

### 4. Industry Conferences & Events

**National Community Development Association (NCDA) Conference**
- Attend and network with development professionals
- Distribute job summary cards
- Conduct informal interviews with qualified candidates
- Timeline: Next conference (typically fall)

**National Housing Conference**
- Similar approach to NCDA
- Target: Housing professionals, developers, capital operators
- Timeline: Next conference

**CDFI Coalition Conference**
- Sponsor or exhibit booth (if budget allows)
- Distribute materials and conduct interviews
- Network with CDFI leaders
- Timeline: Next conference

**Affordable Housing Finance & Development Conference**
- Network with capital professionals and developers
- Distribute materials
- Timeline: Next conference

### 5. Direct Outreach to Key Contacts

**Tier 1: Warm Introductions**
- Leverage board members, advisors, and existing network
- Request introductions to qualified capital professionals
- Personal touch increases response rates
- Timeline: Immediate

**Tier 2: Cold Outreach**
- Identify capital professionals through LinkedIn, industry directories
- Send personalized emails or LinkedIn messages
- Include brief overview and link to careers page
- Timeline: Week 1–4

**Tier 3: Referral Incentives (Optional)**
- Offer referral bonus for successful hires ($5,000–$10,000)
- Share with board, advisors, and network
- Incentivizes active sourcing
